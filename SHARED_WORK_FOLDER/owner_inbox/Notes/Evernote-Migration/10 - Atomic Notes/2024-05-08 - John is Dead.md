just finished reading T's three pages of musings about John, the drugs, the betrayal, the missed chances. i had to write something as well because that sting (the sonofabitch!) while buried deep is just a scabbed over scar.

many times i have thought that John made such a sad choice when he elected to live in the drugged world when that would of course mean that he lived out of the loving relationship world that was there--but disintegrating fast by the end. back in berkeley he had people who cared about him. and not just some knuckleheads who were just scraping by, but some really high quality people that could have helped him (as he was just scraping by so much of the time). but no, he traded that for the escape, the good feeling that the drugs could provide.

T and I were just talking about the phrase "he did the best he could". that phrase evokes two strong feelings in me that apply quite clearly to John:

1.  

    ☐

    yes, i know you could not handle things any other way because you were so broken emotionally and handicapped genetically...

2.  

    ☐

    but you didn't even *try* to do the work it would take to dig yourself out of that self-pitying hole--you just took the easy way (although that path would prove to be less than an easy ride) and took no responsibility for any of it

several times i considered (very briefly) making contact with him to try to bridge the chasm that had formed when he disappeared into alcohol and cocaine. the idea was fleeting because (just as Terese has described in her pages) nothing really had changed. there would be lies. there would be excuses. there would be excellent, unavoidable reasons why everything in his life happened just the way it did. it didn't take much consideration to conclude that such a meeting would only lead to more pain and frustration.

so, goodbye, John. we were the best of friends once--and then you disappeared into a cravasse so deep i would literally never see you again.

and now i wonder: what feelings did it bring up for you when you thought back to those nights we played our guitars together, writing music that wasn't very good but was somewhat cathartic? how did you remember the months we lived in Seattle, somehow playing at living because we didn't really know how yet to be real people? and, in the end, when you drove away from our townhouse, did you know that you would never see any of us again? did that break your heart or did that just seem like the way it had to go--because you were always the victim of circumstance and none of it was ever really your fault.
