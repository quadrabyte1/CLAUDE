# Mother's Day 2026 — Charming Shopping Towns Day-Trip from West Hartford

**Trip date:** Sunday, May 10, 2026 (Mother's Day)
**Home base:** West Hartford, CT
**Target radius:** ~75 miles / ~90 minute drive
**Researcher:** Pax (Senior Researcher)

---

## If I had to pick one for *your* mom

**Essex, CT** is the safest, most-charming, lowest-friction pick. Drive time is reasonable (~50 min), the village is genuinely walkable end-to-end, the shops skew toward the established / craft / independent end (no chains), and the Griswold Inn's famous Hunt Breakfast Sunday brunch is the kind of meal a mom remembers. Pair it with a stroll down Main Street to the river and a stop at Olive Oyl's or the Connecticut River Museum for a scenic finish. **Reserve the Gris this week** — Mother's Day fills.

If your mom skews **artsy/curated/design** rather than coastal/historic, swap in **Kent, CT** — same drive time, more galleries and craft, less seafood-y, very mom-friendly walkable strip with House of Books and R.T. Facts as anchors.

If she's a **serious antiques person**, the answer is **Woodbury, CT** — but understand it's a Sunday-iffy town for shop hours; better as a Saturday plan.

## The 7 Candidates

### 1. Essex, CT
- **Drive time from West Hartford:** ~50 min (about 35 miles, down Rt 9)
- **The pitch:** A perfectly preserved Connecticut River seaport village — clapboard houses, a Main Street that ends at the water, and the oldest continuously-operating inn in America.
- **Shopping vibe:** Coastal-preppy + nautical + craft + small-batch food; not antique-heavy. Boutiques over barns.
- **Signature stops:**
  - **The Griswold Inn Store** (across from the inn) — gifts, nautical-leaning home goods, "goods & curiosities"
  - **Emmy's on Main** and **One N Main** — women's clothing boutiques
  - **The Connecticut River Artisans Co-op** — 30+ regional crafters
  - **The Glass Basket** — gifts and glass ornaments
  - **Olive Oyl's** (gourmet provisions / sandwiches / takeaway treats)
- **Lunch pick:** **The Griswold Inn**, 36 Main St — Sunday Hunt Breakfast buffet (10am-2pm in past years), called by the NYT "the most famous brunch in Connecticut." **Reservation required for Mother's Day; book immediately via OpenTable or call (860) 767-1776.**
- **Sunday / Mother's Day caveats:** Most of the village is open Sunday — Essex is tourism-aware. Mother's Day brunch at the Gris books out; if it's full, the Wine Bar & Bistro at the Griswold is a fallback.
- **Parking + walkability:** Free public parking lot on Main Street next to the Griswold. The whole strip is roughly 4 walkable blocks ending at Steamboat Dock — easy on any mom.
- **What makes it different:** It's the only river/water option on this list. You get the village + the view.

### 2. Kent, CT
- **Drive time from West Hartford:** ~1 hr 15 min (about 55 miles via Rt 4 / Rt 7)
- **The pitch:** A gallery-and-boutique village in the Litchfield Hills, more design-curated and less stuffy than Litchfield itself. The "art town" of northwest CT.
- **Shopping vibe:** Galleries, design-forward home, books, craft, antique-lite. Skews younger and more contemporary than Woodbury or Essex.
- **Signature stops:**
  - **House of Books**, 10 N Main St — beloved independent bookstore in a historic building
  - **R.T. Facts**, 22 S Main St — eclectic antique furniture, lighting, garden, architectural salvage (a destination in itself)
  - **Morrison Gallery**, 60 N Main St — major contemporary art gallery; **open Sunday 12-4**
  - **Foreign Cargo Gallery / Kent Art Association** — rotating shows from regional artists
  - **The Kent Collection / Kent Green** — cluster of small shops near the green
- **Lunch pick:** **Fife 'n Drum Restaurant & Inn** — Sunday opening at 9am, fine dining with live piano; long-running family-run institution. **(Call to confirm Mother's Day reservation availability — books up.)** Casual alt: **Kingsley Tavern** for a less-formal Sunday lunch.
- **Sunday / Mother's Day caveats:** Kent is genuinely open Sunday — galleries and shops have learned that's their busy day. House of Books and Morrison are both Sunday-confirmed.
- **Parking + walkability:** Street parking on Main + Kent Green lot. The walkable strip is ~3 blocks but dense; very mom-friendly.
- **What makes it different:** The most curated/contemporary feel of the CT towns — more like a Hudson Valley vibe but closer.

---

### 3. Litchfield, CT
- **Drive time from West Hartford:** ~45 min (about 28 miles, west on Rt 4 / Rt 202)
- **The pitch:** The classic New England town green, white-clapboard, historic, slightly aristocratic. Antiques + a few sharp boutiques. The most "iconic Connecticut" of the list.
- **Shopping vibe:** Antiques + lifestyle-luxury boutique mix. Established, refined, not flashy.
- **Signature stops:**
  - **Jeffrey Tillou Antiques**, 39 West St — three-story Americana / 18th-19th c. furniture; **open Sunday 11:30-4:30**
  - **South Street Antiques** — period-furniture specialists on the Green
  - **HTC Home** — vintage and custom home furnishings
  - **R Derwin Clothiers** / Litchfield's small lifestyle boutiques around the Green
  - **Arethusa Farm Dairy** (just outside town in Bantam) — cheese, ice cream, milk; one of the best dairy stops in CT
- **Lunch pick:** **West Street Grill**, 43 West St — refined New American, lunch served Sat & Sun 11:30am-3:30pm; longtime Litchfield favorite that does a real Mother's Day brunch service. **Reserve.** Italian alt: **Materia Ristorante** (call for Sunday).
- **Sunday / Mother's Day caveats:** Tillou is confirmed Sunday open; many smaller Litchfield shops are not. The Green itself stays "alive" Sunday but expect 60-70% of the strip open vs Saturday's full slate.
- **Parking + walkability:** Park around the Green or on West Street. Compact, all flat, easy walk.
- **What makes it different:** The only true classic-CT-town-green-postcard option. If "charming little town" means the picture in your head of New England, this is it.

---

### 4. New Preston, CT (with Lake Waramaug)
- **Drive time from West Hartford:** ~1 hr 5 min (about 45 miles)
- **The pitch:** A jewel-box village of about a dozen design-forward shops above a 40-foot waterfall, with Lake Waramaug ten minutes up the road. Tiny but punches way above its weight.
- **Shopping vibe:** Design / interiors / lifestyle-curated. The most Hamptons-meets-Litchfield-Hills feel on the list.
- **Signature stops:**
  - **J. Seitz & Co.**, in the historic mill building — 5,000 sq ft of furniture, women's/men's apparel, home decor; the anchor of the village (39+ years in business)
  - **Dawn Hill Antiques**, 11 Main St — Swedish/European antiques
  - **Pergola**, 7 East Shore Rd — garden, home, plants
  - **Privet House**, 13 East Shore Rd — interiors / table / linen
  - **Plain Goods**, 1 New Preston Hill Rd — apparel / home / curated lifestyle
- **Lunch pick:** **The Owl Wine & Food Bar**, 18 East Shore Rd — Sunday brunch noon-3pm in past schedules. **(Call to confirm Sunday hours and Mother's Day reservation.)** Alt scenic: drive 5 min to **Lake Waramaug** and picnic from village provisions, or **Hopkins Inn** lakeside (seasonal — call).
- **Sunday / Mother's Day caveats:** Sunday hours vary shop to shop here — the high-design stores often run Thurs-Sun, so Sunday is fine, but the village is small enough that you'll exhaust the shops in 90 minutes. **Pair this with Lake Waramaug for the day to make sense.** Note: the original Oliva Cafe at the waterfall closed; The Owl is the current operator at that address.
- **Parking + walkability:** Street/lot parking; flat 2-block walk. A genuinely tiny footprint.
- **What makes it different:** The lake. None of the other towns gives you a postcard lake drive 5 minutes from the shopping. Best if mom likes a "drive + view" component.

### 5. Stockbridge, MA (Berkshires)
- **Drive time from West Hartford:** ~1 hr 20 min (about 75 miles, up Rt 7 / Mass Pike)
- **The pitch:** Norman Rockwell's town — the actual Main Street he painted. Quintessential Berkshires village with the Red Lion Inn at its heart.
- **Shopping vibe:** Country store + craft + small boutique cluster (compact). Less dense than Lenox; more iconic.
- **Signature stops:**
  - **Williams & Sons Country Store**, 36 Main St — 200+ years old, the storefront in Rockwell's "Home for Christmas"
  - **The Mews** — small cluster of shops behind Main Street
  - **Peace, Love & Chocolate** (in The Mews) — cafe & chocolate
  - **Prado de Lana Sheep Farm shop** — local wool / yarn
  - **Norman Rockwell Museum gift shop** (15 min away on Rt 183 — pair with the museum visit if mom is a fan)
- **Lunch pick:** **The Red Lion Inn**, 30 Main St — **Mother's Day brunch confirmed Sunday May 10, 2026, 11am-3pm.** Reservations via OpenTable or (413) 298-5545. **They flag spots filling fast — book this week.**
- **Sunday / Mother's Day caveats:** Stockbridge proper is small — a single Main Street block of shops. Plan to combine it with Lenox (15 min north, denser shopping) or the Rockwell Museum to make a full day. Otherwise you'll be done shopping in 45 min.
- **Parking + walkability:** Street parking on Main; flat, easy.
- **What makes it different:** The Rockwell connection is unique. If your mom's mom-vibe is "remember when America looked like that," this is the one.

---

### 6. Lenox, MA (Berkshires) — pair-with-Stockbridge or standalone
- **Drive time from West Hartford:** ~1 hr 25 min (about 75 miles)
- **The pitch:** The denser, more upscale Berkshires village — more shops, more restaurants, real boutique cluster, walkable downtown.
- **Shopping vibe:** Upscale boutiques + home/lifestyle + jewelry + a few galleries. A real shopping-day downtown, not a one-street stop.
- **Signature stops:**
  - **Twigs** — long-running upscale women's boutique
  - **CERI** — designer apparel & accessories
  - **MacKimmie Co.** — American/European-made textiles, gifts, apparel, home
  - **Laurie Donovan Designs** — fine jewelry / custom design
  - **Catwalk** — Church Street, fashion with proceeds to Berkshire Humane Society (a charming hook)
- **Lunch pick:** **Haven Cafe and Bakery**, 8 Franklin St — popular brunch spot, elevated menu, warm atmosphere. **Brava** (tapas) and **Alta** (Mediterranean) are stronger sit-down dinner options if you stay later. **(Call to confirm Mother's Day reservation — Lenox brunch books out.)**
- **Sunday / Mother's Day caveats:** Lenox is genuinely Sunday-busy — the downtown is built for tourism. Most boutiques open Sunday, brunch is the bottleneck.
- **Parking + walkability:** Public lot off Church St + street; very walkable downtown grid.
- **What makes it different:** Of the Berkshires options, this is the one where the shopping itself is the point. Pair with Stockbridge (15 min south) for a one-two punch and you've got the best Berkshires day-trip on this list — but it IS the longest drive.

---

### 7. Rhinebeck, NY (Hudson Valley)
- **Drive time from West Hartford:** ~1 hr 50 min — *the outer edge of "day trip"*
- **The pitch:** Hudson Valley's most famously charming village — America's oldest inn (Beekman Arms, 1766), a barn full of antiques behind it, and a Main Street of independent boutiques.
- **Shopping vibe:** Hudson Valley = hipper, more design-forward, more food-artisan than CT. Less preppy, more curated.
- **Signature stops:**
  - **Beekman Arms Antique Market**, 28 Market St — 30 dealers in the converted livery barn behind the inn, **open daily 11-5**
  - **Rhinebeck Department Store** / Market Street boutique cluster
  - **Krause's Chocolates** — local chocolatier institution
  - **Oblong Books & Music** — strong independent bookstore (Rhinebeck branch)
  - **Terrapin** — wine/provisions
- **Lunch pick:** **The Tavern at the Beekman Arms** — Sunday brunch served, lunch & dinner 7 days/year-round. Historic tavern setting. **Reserve for Mother's Day.**
- **Sunday / Mother's Day caveats:** Rhinebeck is fully Sunday-open — it's a weekend tourism town. The drive is the watch-out.
- **Parking + walkability:** Free municipal lot off Market; walkable grid.
- **What makes it different:** It's a different *vibe* — Hudson Valley tastes (more design-y, more food-artisan, more "Brooklyn-graduated") rather than Litchfield County preppy or Berkshires inn-y. If mom reads dwell or watches Bake Off, lean here.

## Towns I considered and cut (and why)

- **Woodbury, CT** — antiques capital, 35+ shops, but **a meaningful share of the antique barns are closed Sunday**. The town is gloriously made for a Saturday. Don't burn Mother's Day on it; come back.
- **Chester, CT** — wonderful, walkable, but its signature **Sunday Market doesn't start until mid-June**, and its "wow" is the market. Pair as a 30-min add-on to Essex if you want.
- **Madison, CT** — R.J. Julia (open Sundays 10-6) is genuinely great, but the rest of downtown Madison is thin. Use as a stop, not a destination.
- **Mystic / Stonington Borough, CT** — too far (2 hrs+) for a Mother's Day where you also want a leisurely lunch.
- **Putnam, CT** — Antiques Marketplace is excellent (open Sunday) but the surrounding downtown is much smaller than Woodbury or Litchfield, and it's a 50-min drive east into the Quiet Corner. Niche pick if mom is an antiques hunter.
- **Hudson, NY (Warren St)** — a stronger antiques street than Rhinebeck and more design-y, but it's 2+ hours and a much longer day. **Save Hudson for an overnight, not Mother's Day.**
- **Northampton, MA** — fun, but more "college town energy" than "charming little town." Wrong vibe for a Mother's Day shopping day.
- **Great Barrington, MA** — strong shopping (Bookloft, Westerlind, Dottie's), pair with Stockbridge/Lenox if you go Berkshires; standalone it's no better than Lenox.
- **Newport, RI / Wickford, RI** — too far for a sane Mother's Day round-trip.
- **Williamstown / Concord / Cold Spring** — all >2 hrs; cut on travel time.

## Logistical notes for Larry to flag to the homeowner

1. **Reserve lunch this week. Today, ideally.** Mother's Day brunch at the Griswold Inn, the Red Lion Inn, and West Street Grill all book solid 1-2 weeks out. As of today (May 5) you have 5 days.
2. **Sunday hours are real.** This list has been built around towns that mostly stay open Sundays, but for any specific shop on the list it's still worth a quick call Thursday/Friday.
3. **Weather check Friday/Saturday.** Lake Waramaug and Essex (river views) are weather-dependent in a way that Litchfield and Lenox aren't.
4. **Mother's Day traffic on Rt 7 north (Berkshires) and the Mass Pike west is heavier than a normal Sunday.** Add 15 minutes both ways if going to Stockbridge/Lenox.
5. **Cash for antique stalls.** Beekman Arms Antique Market and many Litchfield/Woodbury barns prefer or require cash for sub-$50 items.
