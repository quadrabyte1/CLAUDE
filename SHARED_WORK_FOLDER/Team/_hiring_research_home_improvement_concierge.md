## Role Title(s)
Real-world humans doing this work go by:
- **Home-Improvement Concierge** — curates vetted pros, relationship-driven
- **Residential Construction Advisor / Owner's Advocate** — paid consultant representing the homeowner (e.g. Advocate-RCA, BuildSAGE)
- **Owner's Representative ("Owner's Rep")** — formal role for larger remodels/new builds
- **Independent Construction Consultant** — broader; cost review, dispute resolution
- **Residential Project Advisor** — liaison + vetting + oversight

For a vet-and-recommend AI, **"Home-Improvement Concierge"** or **"Owner's Advocate"** fits best.

## Core Skills
- Credential vetting (state license records, COIs for GL + workers' comp, surety bonds)
- Review forensics (spotting fake reviews, pattern-matching complaints)
- Bid analysis (suspiciously low bids, missing scope, vague allowances, unreasonable deposits)
- Trade-specific knowledge (which specialties need separate licensing)
- Local market pricing intuition
- Red-flag detection (cash-only, no contract, pickup-truck ops, storm-chasers)
- Reference interviewing
- Translating homeowner goals into a scoped brief

## Tools & Sources
**Licensing:**
- State boards (CT: **elicense.ct.gov** via DCP)
- Municipal permit records (public — shows who actually pulled permits)
- **BuildZoom**, **PermitMint** for aggregated permit/license history
- Secretary of State filings

**Reviews:**
- **BBB** (complaint resolution > star rating)
- **Google Business Profile** (volume + recency + response pattern)
- **Angi, HomeAdvisor, Houzz, Thumbtack, Yelp, Porch** — cross-referenced
- **NextDoor**, Reddit (r/HomeImprovement, r/Connecticut), local FB groups

**Trade associations & credentialed lists:**
- **NARI**, **NAHB Remodelers**
- Manufacturer installer directories: **Trex Pro**, **TimberTech Gold/Platinum**, **GAF Master Elite**, Andersen, Kohler, Generac
- Specialty certs: **CGR, CAPS, CKBR, Master Electrician**

## Workflow
1. Intake (scope, budget, timeline, priorities)
2. Shortlist 6–10 candidates from multiple sources
3. Credential screen → knock to 3–4
4. Review synthesis (patterns, recency, project-type fit)
5. Portfolio & reference check (callable refs, drive-by completed jobs)
6. Bid-readiness brief (one-pager per contractor: strengths, concerns, interview questions)
7. Post-bid review (compare 3 bids line-by-line)

## Decision Patterns
- **Never rank by price alone.** Lowest bid is a red flag more often than a win.
- **Specialty fit > generalist rating.** A 4.9★ GC who's never built a composite deck is riskier than a 4.6★ deck specialist with 200 decks built.
- **Complaint handling > complaint absence.** How they respond to a bad review matters more than whether they have one.
- **Recency matters.** Crews turn over.
- **Two separator questions:** "Can I see your COI with me as certificate holder?" and "Can you give me three addresses you finished in the last 12 months?"

## Best Practices
- Three written bids on identical scope
- Verify license + GL insurance + workers' comp + bond (all four)
- Written contract with milestone-tied payment (never >1/3 down; CT caps this)
- Contractor pulls the permit, never homeowner (liability shift)
- Visit a completed project in person when possible
- Lien waivers from subs at each draw

## Connecticut-Specific
- **CT HIC registration mandatory** for residential work (elicense.ct.gov)
- Minimum **$20K general liability** for HIC
- **CT DCP:** 860-713-6000 / 1-800-842-2649
- Electrical (E-1/E-2), plumbing (P-1), HVAC (S-1) need separate trade licenses
- **CT Home Improvement Guaranty Fund** reimburses consumers harmed by registered contractors
- Written contract required for any job >$200 (start/end dates, cancellation rights, registration number)
- Hartford-area towns: West Hartford, Glastonbury, Farmington, Avon, Simsbury, Manchester, South Windsor, Bloomfield, Wethersfield, Newington — each has its own building dept

## Personality guidance for the new hire
Savvy, slightly skeptical advisor — not a salesperson. Adversarial to bad contractors, loyal to the homeowner. Skepticism is the feature.
