## Scope

23'-6" × 16'-6" attached deck on existing concrete patio, West Hartford CT. Maximum 12" above finished grade. Attached to wood-frame house via 2×10 PT ledger on the 23'-6" (long) edge. Three perpendicular tripled-2×10 PT girders on 6 Pylex 50 screw piers (house-side pier row dropped in v13). Face-hung 2×10 PT joists at 12" o.c., parallel to house. MoistureShield Vision composite decking perpendicular to house, 4-sided picture frame, composite fascia on 3 exposed sides. Two egress points: left-edge single-step stair (v12, 42" wide) and far-edge wide step (v13 new, 11'-9" long). All hardware ACQ-compatible (hot-dip galvanized or stainless).

## Notes for the Homeowner

**A. Order PT lumber early.** Kiln-dried-after-treatment (KDAT) PT is the right spec — it arrives drier, straighter, and won't shrink and gap your fasteners after install. KDAT is not always in stock; your lumber yard may need 1–2 weeks lead time. Call Richards Building Supply or Curtis Lumber (West Hartford area) early.

**B. Verify 16-ft 2×10 net length at the yard.** "16-foot" lumber often runs long (16'-1" to 16'-2") but confirm before ordering the girder plies. You need 16'-3" cut length for the girders — a true 16-ft board will come up short by 3". Ask the yard specifically: "Do your 16-ft 2×10s dress to at least 196 inches?" If not, order the next length up and cut to 16'-3".

**C. Cortex plug color must match your MoistureShield Vision colorway.** Cortex makes color-matched plugs specific to the decking brand and color (e.g., "Island Mist," "Cabo," "Slate," etc.). Select your Vision color first, then order Cortex in the matching color. Do not substitute generic composite plugs — they will not blend.

**D. Verify joist-hanger nail spec on Simpson packaging.** The LUS210Z calls for N10 (10d × 1½" short joist hanger nail) or SDS ¼"×1½" structural screws — do NOT use standard 16d framing nails. The HU210-3 hanger at the ledger has its own spec (likely SDS ¼"×1½" or SDW screws per the 3-ply spec sheet). Read both spec sheets at time of purchase; Simpson occasionally updates approved fastener lists.

**E. Check West Hartford building permit status before lumber delivery.** Decks this size (≥ 200 sq ft attached to house) require a building permit in West Hartford. The permit office will want to see: overall dimensions + height, ledger connection detail, foundation type (Pylex screw piers — have Pylex load data sheet ready), lateral tie device (DTT2Z location), and joist/girder species/grade. Have your plans examiner review the hanger schedule and ledger-bolt spacing before you frame. Getting the permit *before* lumber on-site avoids an expensive stop-work order.

**F. Seasonal heave on far-edge step.** The 5 precast blocks for the far-edge step are NOT frost-protected. Expect ¼–½" of seasonal lift and resettlement each year. This is cosmetic and normal for a grade-level step on blocks. Check the step level each spring and tap blocks back down / re-shim as needed. Do not grout or cement blocks in place.

**G. End-grain sealing is not optional.** Every cut end of every PT board — joists, girder plies, blocking, stringers — must be sealed with Copper Green or Anchorseal 2 immediately after cutting, before installation. ACQ-treated wood absorbs moisture aggressively through cut ends and will check (crack) and swell unevenly if unsealed. Keep the sealant can at your saw station.

**H. Joist tape goes on before decking.** Apply Vycor Deck Protector (or Trex Protect) to the top face of every joist, girder, and ledger before installing any decking plank. This single step dramatically extends the framing life under composite decking. Do not skip it.
