It came up yesterday that the term 'event' as used in software requirements is

- ☐

  a very useful concept, and 

- ☐

  easily confused with all the other definitions of that word we use every day as software engineers.

I'm a strong believer in the importance of precise communication and the role of good vocabulary to that end. As Alan Cooper argues in his book *The Inmates Are Running the Asylum* (among other places), unambiguous communication is so important it doesn't even matter if a newly adopted term makes linguistic sense. In fact, sometimes its better if it doesn't make sense because that way when a term is used (once it has been accepted by the team as legitimate) everyone knows *exactly* what is meant.

\

In that spirit I offer four candidates for words to adopt in place of 'event' in the context of a software requirement (in order of my preference):

- ☐

  Post (or Posting)

  - ☐

    *The software shall *issue *a  TELEOP_ENGAGE_REQUEST *****post**** *upon *detecting* a UID_INSTRUMENT_GRIP_MATCH *****post**** 

- ☐

  Snap

  - ☐

    *The software shall *issue *a  TELEOP_ENGAGE_REQUEST *****snap**** *upon *detecting* a UID_INSTRUMENT_GRIP_MATCH *****snap**** 

- ☐

  Meta-event

  - ☐

    *The software shall *issue *a  TELEOP_ENGAGE_REQUEST *****meta-event**** *upon *detecting* a UID_INSTRUMENT_GRIP_MATCH *****meta-event**** 

- ☐

  Occurrence

  - ☐

    *The software shall *issue *a  TELEOP_ENGAGE_REQUEST *****occurrence**** *upon *detecting* a UID_INSTRUMENT_GRIP_MATCH *****occurrence**** 

Thoughts?
