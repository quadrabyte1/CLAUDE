I stopped by to talk about this but you weren't around so here's a brief answer to the question I think you are asking:

- ☐

  There is an engineering purpose to this diagram. We simply don't have such a thing--which I consider a significant shortcoming so I have already spent lots of hours trying to correct the problem. But I don't have all the answers when it comes to describing in a form like this *all* the details of a system as complex as ours. I have to get help from the people that know the details. 

- ☐

  I have spoken to about a dozen engineers about my idea to put this diagram up in public places within Verb and to encourage everyone to make comments on the drawing so I can make changes that reflect the way things really are. Everyone I talked to about this thought it was a very good idea, including John Magnasco this morning.

- ☐

  I have already had a half dozen requests from people for a copy of the diagram because they thought it would be useful on their walls.

- ☐

  I didn't get approval for any of this from anyone because I didn't see a need for such approval.

- ☐

  I am not convinced that there is much in the way of confidential or trade secret information provided by this diagram. In the very worst case, it might be possible for someone to glean some useful tidbits of information from this diagram, but *only if that person was to stand there and study it for tens of minutes.* I can tell you from experience that even people with deep knowledge about various parts of the system don't "get it" right away when first introduced to the drawing. It takes even them several minutes of study to discern what is being conveyed.

- ☐

  As to the question I think you are asking about other copies, yes, I printed and posted 6 copies of this diagram this morning. One in my office. One I gave to Joelle Barral, at her request, to post somewhere in Goggle Land (to which I have no access), and four of them in highly visible places in four of our five buildings.

- ☐

  Lastly, even if you are right that sensitive information is shown on the diagram, surely we don't allow people who have not signed at least an NDA with Verb to wander the halls and study anything they might find lying around.

Thomas

Jim,

\

I'm still spending lots of time on the problem of nailing down a *comprehensive* software module list. As you know, several teams are somewhat hamstrung by the fact that we still don't have one. This task is very high priority--and happily not too far from wrapping up but it isn't done yet.

\

It has taken a long time to get to the conclusion that the list I have requested from Nick is really the best way to derive (over and over again to keep up with changes that will inevitably occur as things evolve) a complete, reliable set of executable things that are poured into the hardware to get the system to perform as needed. With the proviso that the FPGA software elements are not yet going to be listed because they haven't been integrated yet, that list of executable things is also a reliable checklist of all the modules we need to account for.

\

This is not an off-the-cuff request. I have been asking for this very thing for several months now but, until someone pointed me to Nick to ask some related questions, it didn't seem possible to get this information from anyone. The progress that has been made on this module list so far has been entirely from the archaeological work of piecing together all the relatively small pieces of the puzzle provided by people like you. Good progress, but assembling the bigger picture this way is not guaranteed to account for everything--and *everything* has to be the goal for regulatory reasons. 

\

It doesn't matter to me if Nick does the work to produce the list or someone else. What does matter is that the list be generated and preferably in some automatically repeated way so that an automation tool can double check that the latest module list is still in sync with the latest set of installed things.

\
