# MoistureShield vs Trex — Dimensional Comparison for v10 Deck Design

**Researcher:** Pax
**Date:** 2026-05-01
**Project:** West Hartford deck (23'-6" × 16'-6", v10)

---

## Verdict: SIGNIFICANT REWORK REQUIRED

Switching from Trex to MoistureShield is **not a drop-in substitution**. Every MoistureShield decking line (Vision, Meridian, Elevate, Vantage) is **5.4" wide × 1" thick** vs Trex's **5.5" × 0.94"**. Their fascia is **0.67" × 11.25"** vs the assumed 0.75" Trex fascia. Both deltas cascade through the v10 picture-frame geometry and plank count.

If Thomas wants a true drop-in dimensional match, he should stay with Trex (or another 5.5"/0.94" board such as TimberTech AZEK, Deckorators, or Fiberon ProTect — most premium capped composites in this class are 5.5"). MoistureShield's choice of 5.4" is a deliberate brand-specific dimension; it is consistent across all their decking lines.

## Important fascia note

The v10 design lists "0.75" Trex Universal Fascia." That is **only correct for the Trex PVC TrexTrim** product (1" × 12" PVC fascia, actual 0.75" × 11.25"). The actual **Trex Universal composite fascia** is **0.56" × 11.375"** — thinner. If Thomas truly intended a composite fascia (not PVC), the v10 baseline was already off by 0.19" before MoistureShield entered the picture. Worth flagging back to the design.

MoistureShield's 0.67" composite fascia falls between the two Trex options — closer to a true composite than to the PVC.

## Design impact on the v10 plan

### 1. Plank count changes (5.5" → 5.4")
v10 currently uses **48 field planks** at 5.5" + 1/8" gap = 5.625" pitch across the 22'-7" (271") field width.

With MoistureShield's 5.4" board + 1/8" gap = 5.525" pitch:
- 271" / 5.525" = **49.05 planks → 49 planks** (with a slim final gap), or hold at 48 and widen the gap to 0.27" (too wide — visible).

**Recommendation:** Re-plot at 49 planks, 5.4" wide, 1/8" gap. This also slightly changes the location of the labeled plank indices (1, 12, 24, 36, 48 → 1, 13, 25, 37, 49).

### 2. PF picture-frame width changes (5.5" → 5.4")
The four PF perimeter boards are also 5.4" wide instead of 5.5". The framing footprint back-calculation (23'-0" × 16'-3") needs to re-derive from:
- Outer dim (23'-6" × 16'-6") − 2 × fascia − 2 × PF overhang
- Or Outer dim − 2 × PF outboard projection past framing

Net change: framing outline shrinks by **0.2"** in each dimension (was 23'-0" × 16'-3"; becomes ≈ 23'-0.2" × 16'-3.2" if PF overhang is held constant — within typical construction tolerance, but the drawing labels should be updated).

### 3. Fascia thickness changes (0.75" → 0.67")
PF overhang past fascia is currently 2.25". If you hold the **outer fascia face** at the same location (so the deck's outer footprint stays 23'-6" × 16'-6"), then framing moves outward 0.08" on each side. If you hold the **framing in place**, the outer footprint shrinks 0.16" each direction. Either is within construction tolerance but the dimension callouts on the plan change.

### 4. Decking thickness changes (0.94" → 1.0")
Top-of-deck rises 0.06" — **negligible**, well within the 12"-above-grade hard constraint.

### 5. Joist spacing — no change
v10 already uses 12" o.c. (homeowner spec). MoistureShield only requires 16" o.c. perpendicular / 12" o.c. for diagonal. The existing 12" o.c. exceeds MoistureShield's residential requirement — fully compliant.

### 6. Hidden fasteners — no change
v10 doesn't lock in a fastener brand. The Trex Hideaway clip is Trex-only. For MoistureShield, the typical pairing is **FastenMaster Tiger Claw TC-G + Cortex color-match plugs** for the perimeter. Both are widely available in CT.

## Recommended MoistureShield product (closest analog to Trex Transcend)

**MoistureShield Vision** — capped composite, DiamondDefense scratch coating, 50-year fade & stain warranty. This is the direct premium-tier competitor to Trex Transcend. Vision and Meridian share the exact same 5.4" × 1" dimensions, so the dimensional rework is identical regardless of which Vision-tier line Thomas picks.

If budget is a concern, **Meridian** (40-year warranty, TruTexture wood-grain) or **Elevate** (30-year, more affordable) are the same board geometry — only the cap aesthetic and warranty differ.

Avoid **Vantage** for an exposed residential deck — it's uncapped and aesthetically lower-tier (no fade/stain warranty).
