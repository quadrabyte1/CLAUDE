**Preliminary Summary of Verb-Verily Security Processes and Capability Assessment**

\

**Verb has an obligation to ensure processes, risks, design and monitoring are all meet regulatory requirements. It is imperative that we maintain a robust cybersecurity program not only for our products, but also for host hospitals and our patients.  Such a program will be a tremendous amount of work to implement successfully. While Google/Verily are (somewhat) willing to help us to create a well-designed program, neither of those organizations are really in a position to take responsibility for any of this work. After meeting with Mike Newton (one of our two cybersecurity contacts at Verily) it seems pretty clear that we should not expect much more than informed advice from that quarter.** **Verily’s Infosec Review Process (IRP) will be a great help in examining our product for** **vulnerabilities but that review process is run as a check at the *end of development*, not as a tool to guide development of security mitigations.  Moreover, that review process is not designed to address the unique security needs of a medical product like ours.**

\

According to the regulatory requirements and national/international standards with which Verb must comply, the scope of the cybersecurity problem goes well beyond just our products interface to the Internet Cloud. The system is vulnerable through all wireless connections, all wired network connections, and even communication channels confined to shorter physical distances (for example Bluetooth and NFC).  Aligning with this larger scope, Verb’s overall Cybersecurity Plan follows FDA guidance and applicable standards.  The plan, based on the NIST Framework for Improving Critical Infrastructure Cybersecurity, goes into the details of every aspect of the framework core: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. The amount of work required to actually execute the Cybersecurity Plan is considerable; we estimate that at least two cybersecurity-knowledgeable full time people will be needed (starting as soon as possible) to develop the mitigations described in the plan.

Further, we will additional people (possibility with entirely different skill sets) to continuously monitor our products in the field to respond to new threats and vulnerabilities which will certainly arise. Google's and Verily’s Security Engineering Teams, along with its Infosec Review Process, will provide a valuable consulting service in the area of product design analysis and they can help with some of the security testing.  However, we are concerned that neither group's contributions to the effort will cover the needs of routine testing and continuous monitoring in the remaining areas of documentation, risk management, formal V&V and post-market monitoring.

\

**In conclusion, by assessing the requirements and capabilities, more resources, including but not limited to Verily’s security expertise, will be needed to accomplish a successful cybersecurity program for the Verb Surgical System.**  As a next step, we recommend proceeding with detailed assessments of schedule and resource needs, either based on yearly or “design control/phase-wised” work breakdown, for both Verb and Verily, plus potential outsourced firms.
