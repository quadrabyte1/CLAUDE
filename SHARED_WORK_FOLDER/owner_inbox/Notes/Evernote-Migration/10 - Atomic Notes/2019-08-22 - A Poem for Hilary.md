For Monkey

\

soon decades passed

and fathering became,

like breathing, sleeping, eating,

a woven part of him

another bright dimension that went unseen 

but not unnoticed at all

\

knowing it would be both difficult

and the easiest of all life's tasks

he taught them both to fly,

the sparrow and the monkey,

in his own image

with his own words

and his own arms,

because he simply had nothing else at hand

\

looking back to hospital days,

to neophyte fears and stainless steel scissors

It dawns on him, he had it wrong, in the beginning:

the painful thing would not be fledging

as he had dreaded as he cut the cord.

the terrible times would come when they would ache

And cry and stammer

when their anguish would, like resonance, 

bring an upswell of dark feeling

to the father once a son

\

today he knows the rest is nought,

jusy silly clouds of wispy white

that his gems are his wife beside him,

his Monkey shrewdly watching the progression

and his Sparrow nesting restlessly 

while they all wait 

to see the sun rise on the next generation

\
