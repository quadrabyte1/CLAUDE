# Swiss Law & Requirements

There are several important factors that frame assisted dying in Switzerland.

1.  1.  

        ☐

        The Swiss Penal Code allows for assisted suicide, as long as the motive of those assisting is not selfish.

    2.  

        ☐

        Those assisting the person to die need not be a doctor. Anyone can assist as long as their motive is altruistic.

Furthermore under Swiss law, the person requesting the assistance to die must possess:

1.  1.  

        ☐

        decision-making capacity; and

    2.  

        ☐

        they must have ‘control’ or ‘ownership of the action’ over their death (‘Tatherrschaft’ in German). (See Art. 115 StGB of the Swiss Penal Code & the decision of the Swiss Federal Court BGE 133 I 58).

**Pillow List (first draft)**

This document is intended to be the "official" explanation of Thomas's wishes with respect to the question of ending his life because the quality of that life has become so poor that it would be better to just switch off, bringing death perhaps somewhat sooner than the established powers that be would have him die. That is to say, if any of these conditions become true, Thomas would like to die rather than continue living under those conditions.

\

I, Thomas Robert Brennan-Marquez, am composing this list on 16 Apr 2024 while I am quite healthy in both mind and body. My sincere hope is that this will help anyone who might be wondering if it is really time for me to die. Trying to get ahead of that inevitable day's arrival, I am trying to lay down some conditions that I know, at least from my perspective today, are awful enough that I don't want to live any longer. Having said that, I do want to make clear that I completely trust my immediate family members (Terese Brennan-Marquez , Kiel Brennan-Marquez , and Hilary Brennan) to decide when the time has come. They know my feelings about my end of life so I am comfortable leaving the ultimate decision to them, if I am not able to make that decision for myself.

\

One more thing I want to make clear: as of this date, 16 Apr 2024, there are only a few legal ways to end life intentionally and I don't know how that situation may change in the future--but what I do not want is for anyone to run the risk of running afoul of the law to end my life.

\

These are the conditions I wanted to list. All of these descriptions presuppose that the condition will likely not improve. The subjective decision about the odds of improvement I will leave to my immediate family. I sincerely hope I have provided enough detail that the decision will, if/when the time comes, be relatively easy to make. And please know that I appreciate how hard this may be on family members but I also really appreciate that you are trying to do the right thing.

1.  

    ☐

    If I don't know who the people in my family are (let's say if that means even half of the family members) as much as 50% of the time, I won't really be around to realize there is family around. In this situation I think it will be just fine for me to die. If the family wants to keep me around for their own reasons (and assuming that "keeping me around" isn't a huge burden on anyone), that will be okay too--but that choice would be my second choice.

2.  

    ☐

    If I have become feeble enough, ill enough, or disabled enough that the only reasonable option left is to put me in a nursing home, I don't want to keep living any longer. Nursing home here means a facility that specializes in handling many/all of the chores associated with staying alive: eating, urinating, defecating, breathing, etc. I am confident that I do not wish to have these chores handled by any paid service or facility. Indeed, in the case that one or more family members feel a desire/responsibility to handle those chores for me, my preference would be that I just say goodbye and die instead.

3.  

    ☐

    Seems too bad to me that handling bodily waste products (shit and urine) is such a difficult and repulsive task but that is how things are. If I am in a condition that I cannot deal with such waste (especially shit) in approximately the "normal" way I have known for most of my life--sitting on a toilet, pooping into the toilet, then cleaning up with toilet paper or a bidet--I don't want to continue to live.

There will likely be a huge grey area between Death and Life--if, when weighing the circumstances for and against dying, the balance comes out to Life without at least a 20% margin, I want to die. To put it another way, if the answer comes up "maybe" please erase that answer and replace it with "it's time to let him go".

Perhaps I will be lucky enough to just die quietly in my sleep. That is my deepest hope. But just in case that fantasy doesn't come true, thank you so much for dealing with this most painful decision. I hope I have helped with this note.
