Jon Dashner 2017

\

Jon and Laura,

\

It is good to hear that things are going well for you and the boys as we wind up a really unusual year.

\

2017 has been a real roller coaster of year at our house. Yet, even with politics and deep divides in the country bringing out the worst in people from both ends of the political spectrum, our family had some very good moments this year. 

\

Hilary and her husband have found jobs out in Santa Cruz, working in the high tech software world. Both are excited by the every day challenge of software and it's great to see them doing something they love.

\

I have started yet another new job (this is number eleven, I think) at a company making a surgical robot. Very cool technology and a perfect fit for me as a Software Architect. Terese likes to point out that it took me my whole career to find "the perfect job" but I guess that's what most people do, if they are lucky enough to ever find that elusive thing.

\

Terese continues to work at the all-girls school in Palo Alto, working with the daughters of many of the movers-and-shakers of the Silicon Valley. That doesn't always sit well with her (she's really a social worker at heart) but I try to encourage her by pointing out that those girls are many of the leaders of the next generation and she's helping them to see the world and themselves in ways that we really need our leaders (and especially our future female leaders) to see. And her psychotherapy practice is more than full--she keeps taking clients when she really doesn't have any slots available--she just adds hours to her schedule.

\

Kiel had two major positive events in his life this year. He just secured a law professor position at the University of Connecticut. His area of special expertise and interest is in fourth amendment rights, policing technology, and the changes to the judiciary that are coming soon as machines begin to provide better and better ways to take human bias out of the equation. (Of course that's my interpretation; he might describe his work quite differently.)

\

He was also married in our backyard in June. That was a wonderful event and, though it did make me feel old to have two married kids now, I was also filled with an interesting feeling I have never had before: seeing Kiel married brought up a strong sense of completion. Perhaps you've experienced it yourselves--otherwise I suspect it is hard to understand because I can't really explain it better than that. Terese and I looked at each other and said "time to sell the house" that we raised the kids in most of their lives. It just feels like the house and us as parents raising the kids there have finished a huge project. Not that we don't see them and thoroughly enjoy them when we do. And certainly not that we don't feel like parents any longer. It's just that a long, wonderful chapter has closed for us. 

\

A very interesting thing happened this year that I thought, you especially Jon, would appreciate: We have some family friends who are very close and go back more than thirty years. In fact, *we* got married in *their* backyard. And, at the time, their oldest Ryan was an only child of about 18 months. He was the catalyst for me deciding that parenting would be a good thing and so Terese and I started working on that project. Well, now Ryan is 33 and he has a girl friend (maybe even a fiancée but I'm not certain about that yet). Her name is Jenny Dashner and her family is from Iowa. There's a coincidence for you. I thought there might be a connection so I asked a few questions and Jenny called her dad -- turns out we are related. Her dad, Chris I think, knows Jon and sees him at the family reunion every year (I think it's every year) back in Iowa. Wow! I was amazed. The world is a small place.

\

Happy Holidays,

Thomas
