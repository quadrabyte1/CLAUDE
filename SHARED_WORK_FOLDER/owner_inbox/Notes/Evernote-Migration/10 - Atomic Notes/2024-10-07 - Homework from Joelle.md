The thinking voice is powerful. Loud. Self-assured.

The feeling voice (if that is indeed what it is) cannot compete in a head-to-head match up.

But I need to practice quieting the thinking voice, just for a time, just to allow the feelings to seepi under the barriers, through the vents, into the dreamspace.

\

How do I peel back a few of the feeling layers, to reach down and touch the feelings that I know are there but clearly are sequestered carefully away?

\

Alone

There is a lingering feeling that I have been noticing: I am aware of being alone in a way I don't recognize from all my years of life. Not lonely or isolated, or insulated, but singular. Like a climber at the pinnacle of one of those columns in the Utah desert, standing there almost triumphant with nothing and no one to the left, to the right, out in front or behind. A wind whistles, reminding me of where and how I am standing here. It feels both solid (I am not afraid of falling from this great height) but also bleak and forlorned -- nowhere to go from here? No up to continue to pursue. No forward or backward. Even the journey back down (which I must do at some point) seems anticlimactic.

\

Sadness

Still there is a well of sadness that comes to hand when I begin to peel away some feeling layers. Some grief, some disappointment I have not been able to dispell. J would say that's normal, that's part of the human condition. No amount of processing it in therapy will erase the scars that are left behind from all the years of hiding, denying, wishing things were not that way. (As I write those feelings begin to bubble up and my mind goes quickly to: get some work done on the computer, engage that muscle immediately to hold those feelings at bay. I wonder if I still can share those with J now, given this new arrangement of client/therapist, the guide and the adventurer, no longer the confidant to whom I whisper painful, firghtening things.)
