*Sorry it's taken so long to get back to you to say thanks for your support. Turns out (something that totally surprised me when they told me initially but made a lot of sense when I stopped to think about it) it takes quite a bit of the body's energy for the brain to do the work of fixing brain-related problems. Apparently with a* *[subdural hematoma](https://medlineplus.gov/ency/article/000713.htm)* *the recovery period includes a fair amount of fatigue as a result of that work; I've been getting lots of rest but after thinking-related tasks like reading, watching a movie, or working on a cool* *[puzzle](https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/push/id1257416763?mt=8)* *app I found for my iPad, I need to take a nap/rest break for about 90 minutes. Makes for very slow progress but I keep reminding myself that my main task right now is to give my brain every opportunity to improve.*

\

*This morning I woke up feeling really good: well rested after an (almost) uninterrupted night's sleep and ready to write this note to say thanks. I have been deeply touched by all the concern, support, and affection I have received from friends and family as a result of this whole experience. Yesterday I read about a guy who was bitten by a shark last week--he was remarkable in that a couple years ago he had been mauled by a bear...then a year after that he was bit by a poisonous snake...and most recently a shark tried him as a snack. The quote I saw struck me as both profoundly true and appropriate to my own situation: he said "I don't know whether I am the luckiest or the unluckiest guy alive." My experience is much less dramatic of course but it has made me feel pretty lucky to be doing so well. *

\

*Here's a brief update on how well things are going on the recovery front 2.5 weeks after the fact, checking against a list of common problems. This may be more information than you really want or need but just in case you are curious at all:*

\

*Confused or slurred speech*

- ☐

  *No problems with speech at all. I have noticed a bit of difficulty with word finding, especially proper nouns, while talking to people but that seems to be improving a little bit each day.*

*Problems with balance or walking*

- ☐

  *Moving around the house to do normal little tasks like getting a snack, showering, or feeding the dogs has been essentially normal for at least a week now.*

- ☐

  *I've started taking the dog for her 15-20 minute twice-daily walks with no problems except that I am I bit tired when I get back home.* **She** *is very happy though.*

- ☐

  *I've noticed a little dizziness once in a while when I sit up or stand too quickly but not at all while we are out walking.*

*Headache*

- ☐

  *I did have some unpleasant headaches the first couple of weeks but those have subsided significantly. Tylenol and occasionally a cup of coffee were pretty much all I needed to get through those. In the last few days I have been sticking to just the coffee and that seems to be doing the trick.*

*Lack of energy*

- ☐

  *Except for the fatigue after tasks I mentioned above, lack of energy hasn't been a huge problem. Breaks and naps seem to be reliable ways to recharge the internal battery. It does feel a bit like my battery is kind of old so doesn't hold a really great charge--much like batteries in some of my old laptops that needed frequent charging because they would give out pretty quickly.*

*Confusion*

- ☐

  *Except for my usual forgetfulness I don't feel confused at all now. There was a bit of fogginess at first but now it's just what has been normal for the last several decades or so.*

*Seizures, loss of consciousness, nausea, vomiting, weakness, numbness, vision problems*

- ☐

  *Nope.*

*Thanks so much for your concern and expression of support. I have been deeply affected by the outpouring of love from all the people who have reached out to me, Terese, and the rest of the family.*

\

*Thomas*
