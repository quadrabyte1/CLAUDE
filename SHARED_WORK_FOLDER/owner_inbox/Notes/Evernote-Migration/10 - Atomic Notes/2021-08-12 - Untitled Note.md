BTW, there are many "state machine" paradigms out there. Each has its own execution rules and many of them have been developed very formally in the academic/theoretical world. Others have been thrown together more casually in the interest of getting specific things done. As with all of SysML/UML (and EA's support for those standards), there are lots of ways of doing many things--the sort of kitchen-sink view of things--but you do not need to use all those mechanisms/notations. If you are interested in using state machines but Nizam's discussion seems a bit intimidating, there are simpler approaches with ardent advocates (I am one of those). Just let me know if you want to discuss the use of state machines in real medical products; I will be happy to talk your ear off about this subject.
